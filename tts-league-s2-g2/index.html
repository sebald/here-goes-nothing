Coordinate shenanigans again. This time paired with the ability to lock past range three. All to support the two ordnance carriers, which are armed to the teeth. Looking at what I brought. I am still excited about Kylo, Vonreg plus two FOs. Vonreg with Daredevil is just pure madness.

I swapped some upgrades and pilots for this match. I wanted to make the list more effective and only play stuff that has value regardless of the opponent‚Äôs squad.

This means removing the Mag-Pulse Warheads and the Advanced Optics. It is only worth firing the warheads if Vonreg or Kylo is in trouble against another ace or to remove the lock from an ordnance carrier. Too bad my opponent brought not one but two ordnance carriers.

As for the Advanced Optics. Kylo always is the primary target. I am fine with that because it means Vonreg can do his thing and shoot with double mods. But if Kylo runs he doesn‚Äôt need the optics. I find that optics on high initiative ships isn‚Äôt that great anyway. You don‚Äôt know if you need the focus for defense later and a target lock is almost as good for Kylo since he has the force.

This freed-up 10 points which I chose to invest in upgrading the TIE/fo pilots. 10 points is exactly enough for Rivas and Scorch. In my opinion the best TIE/fo named pilots. Rivas is only two points more than a cadet and will get free target locks from Vonreg. And other than a cadet, Scorch can not be ignored. Throwing up to four dice at a target is brutal. He is also I4 and can be an ace in some matchups.

Looking at what the squad has to fly against. Neither Kylo nor Vonreg like to be the recipient of torps and missiles. None of my ships has the health to withstand the firepower of the Star Wings. Combined with the high health count, will make this no easy task.

There are no easy points. I can not expect to go in, destroy a ship and get out, leaving the FOs to die. I have to stick to one target for multiple rounds. Not necessarily the plan I have in mind with this squad. Knowing that the two FOs hold 60 instead of 50 points I need to secure an extra 10 points. A clear disadvantage in investing in better pilots.

I thought my best option was the Star Wings. I wanted to take down one of them as fast as possible and then take half points on my next best option, depending on the game state.

Turn 0

Hannes brought three of the smaller debris as obstacles. I am still not sure why. I guess the Lambdas don‚Äôt care about stress, they are better for him than clouds and of course you usually don‚Äôt bring large rocks when playing two large bases.

I packed the two large gas clouds and the largest rock again. After the match and how the obstacles got placed I am no longer sure that this is the correct choice for the list. Maybe it is just because of the lists I am flying against but dang I want more rocks on the battlefield.

Since I won the bid, I chose Hannes to go first. Clearly a mistake if there is only one real obstacle, the rock.

Hannes tugged the rock in the corner on my side. Great. I grabbed one of the clouds and tried to get as many obstacles in the center of the battle field as I could. Hannes stuck to this plan and put his remaining obstacles, the other cloud and one debris field in the other corners while I created a diagonal lane with the remaining two debris fields. I hoped that this would split the battlefield into two zones and leave no lanes for the Lambdas.

Hannes placed his ships in groups of two. The two Lambdas were facing down the northern edge of the board. The two Star Wings started horizontally right next to them. Sai got assigned the Optimized Prototype. By the looks of Hannes formation the Lambdas would occupy the northern zone of the board, while the Star Wings took care of the southern part of the mat.

I did the usual. FOs in the middle. Kylo and Vonreg start in opposite corners to react to the first few turns of movement. At least that‚Äôs the idea. The only adjustment I made was placing the FOs such that they would fly directly at an obstacle. I wanted the option to bank either way with them. The potential of slamming Star Wings made me wary.

Turn 1

As expected, Hannes slow-rolled the first turn with the Star Wings and used Jendon to acquire locks. Sai and the green Starwing locked Kylo. The brown Starwing locked Vonreg.

My ships all did two straights. This is me going slow, I guess.

In addition, Kylo rolled back to have more options next turn. Vonreg boosted to face down the board and threaten the flank where both Star Wings were fyling. Scorched rolled right so that he and Kylo potentially could go after the shuttles next turn.

At least I hope that was what it looked like. I never planned to face the shuttles directly. Rolling Scorch towards them just gave him a better angle to fly through the obstacles with a three bank next turn.

Turn 2

Hannes did go slow with his Star Wings again. The shuttles strolled further down the northern lane towards Kylo.

Because the shuttles are so slow and need time to get through the obstacles in the center of the board I kicked off my plan to catch the Star Wings without their support. Full throttle with all my ships!

Vonreg did a five straight and rolled left to be out of any arcs. Scorch did a three bank to land between the two debris fields. Rivas passed the rock and debris with a two bank. Even Kylo, trailing behind as usual, advanced in the direction of the Star Wings.

I didn‚Äôt want to go too fast with Kylo. I hoped to bait the shuttles into further trying to hunt down Kylo instead of my other ships. He had two locks on Kylo anyway, I couldn‚Äôt take the risk of engaging with him.

Scorch and the green Starwing exchanged shots through the debris. No damage. Everyone else didn‚Äôt have a shot.

Turn 3

This was the crucial turn for my plan to get to the Star Wings without the shuttles helping out. Hannes slow-rolled the first two turns, hence I wanted to go fast again.

With the Star Wings still trying to catch Vonreg, I dialed in another five straight. With the option to Daredevil boost even slow maneuvers couldn‚Äôt catch him and there was a good chance that at least one of the Star Wings will end up in his arc.

And the brown Star Wing did! As I hoped the debris protected Vonreg. The brown Star Wing couldn‚Äôt turn right to face Vonreg.

With a three bank from Rivas and a five straight from Scorch, they also got in range of the Star Wings. Both were in a good spot to shoot at the brown Star Wing. Only Scorch ended up in a wacky position between the two Star Wings.

Seeing that now I maybe did a mistake here. Even though Scorch‚Äôs shot would have been unmodded, a barrel roll to the right would have brought him out of the arc of all opponents. He would have been in a better position for next turn too. At the time I just wasn‚Äôt scared of the two Star Wings shooting at Scorch. He shot first and I choose violence!

Kylo chose the save route. Now with three locks (!) on him, he did not want to have anything to do with it. He banked left and rolled left to get out of the arcs.

Shooting through the debris Vonreg pinged off one shield from brown using his lock. Scorch activated and rolled four hits, which resulted in three hits. One more than expected.

In return the combined fire power of Star Wings only got rid of Scorch‚Äôs shield.

With the shields gone a double modded Rivas, put a hit/crit into brown. The crit stuck and was a direct hit. The brown Starwing remained with only one hull left.

I traded six damage for one. A very good start for me thanks to some better than average dice. Sprinting everything forward was worth it and catched Hannes off-guard.

The shuttles were closing in on the battle. I blocked brown‚Äôs escape path with a hard one from Rivas. Scorch then bumped into brown, clearing his stress. Two guns that will not shoot this turn, ugh.

Anticipating this happening I chose to bring in Kylo. I wanted to make sure the brown Star Wing is destroyed and I wasn‚Äôt sure if Vonreg alone could do it. I actually wasn‚Äôt certain that Vonreg hat a shot at all.

Turn 5

After a good start, I lost ground. Although I got rid of the brown Star Wing, two of my ships suffered a lot of damage. I couldn‚Äôt lose any points with Kylo. There wasn‚Äôt enough time left to shoot down one of the remaining ships to make up for him. So I crossed my fingers and dialed in a five straight with Kylo, hoping to dodge as many arcs as possible.

Jendon flew over the debris. He got a coordinated focus by Sai, who flew directly behind Jendon. Thanks to his ability, Sai also had a focus.

Vonreg flanked the shuttles and started shooting at Jendon. Because Vonreg rolled left to be out of Sai‚Äôs arc, the obstructed shot into Jendon only removed one shield. Scorch‚Äôs single modded four-dice attack into the green Star Wing pinged off two shields.

However that‚Äôs not quite what happened. Sai‚Äôs shot only showed one eyeball and probably would have been a miss. But since Sai had the Optimized Prototype assigned, Hannes spent the result to flip the damage card: a Panicked Pilot.

Kylo sat there with three stress, the third one from Autothrusters. This was the worst crit I could think of in this situation. There was no way to escape the slamming Star Wing now. Not being able to reposition with Kylo was his death sentence.

With half points on Kylo and Scorch Hannes took the lead, 55 to 48.

Turn 6

The least I could do was try to save Kylo. I dialed in a five straight. It was a desperate move because none of the FOs could block the Star Wing, they both faced in the other direction. Scorch was ionized from the crit anyways so I sent our Rivas to block the shuttles and deny actions. Vonreg turned right, locked Jendon for a strain and took a focus as regular action.

Taking the strain was risky but I also was longing for points. Scorch or Kylo, in the worst case both, would not live until the end of the game. I needed points.

Vonreg stripped off the remaining three shields from Jendon. Only one more health until half points. Both shuttles fired back at Vonreg only removing one shield from the baron, phew. Unmodded dice are great!

Now it was the green Star Wing‚Äôs turn to finally revenge his brother. Another barrage rocket and not so great dice on my side ended the chase. Kylo was gone. Not being able to spend any force to save him, Kylo was removed from the board with all two force available.

Turn 7

I was desperate for points. Hannes extended his lead in the last round to 93. That was a much higher number than what I wanted. Time for a hail mary.

Scorch, back in the fight with one health, tried to turn in and shoot at something. Ideally at Jendon but he was too close. Scorch and Jendon bumped into each other.

Rivas did a three turn left, setting up a block for Sai and hopefully helping out Vonreg getting half points for the Star Wing. The green Star Wing landed directly in front of Rivas.

The double modded but depleted shot from Vonreg into the Star Wing did two damage. Three of the seven health left meant half points, yay!

Hannes took some time to think about his last shots. The Star Wing could shoot at Vonreg and Rivas. Sai had only Vonreg sitting in front of him. Jendon could shoot Rivas or Vonreg from his rear arc.

Sai opened the round of fire, shooting at Vonreg. The focus token prevented any damage on Vonreg. Jendons shot into Vonreg did also no damage. With one shield remaining the baron was holding on to his points.

The Star Wing had a focus and a lock on Rivas. It was finally time for the Advanced Proton Torpedo! Three hits and two crits one-shotted Rivas and the game was over.

Conclusion

I am really happy that I participate in the league. Even though I am now 0:2, I learned a lot playing in this environment. I expect to lose a lot in my first tournament anyway. I don‚Äôt feel bad about it. Especially because I had two great games against even greater guys. It really is true what they say, the X-Wing community is full of great people.

Regarding the game, I find that I got a head start in the first part of the game. My plan throwing everything at the Star Wings worked. I got rid of one while the Lambdas were still on their way to the battle.

But I messed up keeping my guns pointed at the enemy ships afterwards. Sure I destroyed one of the Star Wings, but there was no follow up. Rivas and Scorch didn‚Äôt really have the chance to shoot for the rest of the game.

Scorch did a total of five damage with only two shots, both were four dice attacks. Rivas did four damage. Not that bad for a blocker. Kylo? Well he got the kill shot and had to run for the rest of the game. Vonreg shot five out of seven rounds. That‚Äôs great. He totalled eight damage. Still, it‚Äôs really bad if Vonreg is the only one shooting. With Kylo running, Rivas and Scorch have to fill in. I need to get better at deciding if blocking or having a target is better for the FOs.

Revisiting the positions after the fifth round, I maybe should have turned Kylo in and got a last shot off at one of the shuttles. It certainly would not have won me the game, but I maybe would have gotten half points for Jendon.

As for obstacles, I understand now why some players bring the three big rocks. When you play small and fragile but mobile ships you do not gain that much from the gas clouds compared to your opponent. You both can ignore them. You need something in the way of large and immobile ships that they have to respect. Guess I am overthinking my choice of obstacles for the next game.

Until then! If this trend continues my next opponent will surprise me with a list that has 35 total health. üòÜ
